Allergen Alarm

We simplify the lives of people with food allergies.

As many as 5% of infants less than 3 years have diagnosed food allergies

source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_allergy

As many as 3% - 4% of adults have diagnosed food allergies

source: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_allergy

More than 20% of adults think they have food allergies

source: www.foodallergens.info/Facts/How_Many.html

Population US and Canada: approx. 349 M

4% equals 13,960,000 people

Population EU: approx. 502 M

4% equals 20,080,000 people

Canada, US and EU combined market: 34,040,000

and no application to support...

Allergen Alarm instantly tells you if products in the grocery store are safe for people with allergies. Just scan the bar-code and know the risk for you and your loved ones!

Three plans available:

Free - Scan a barcode and get a list of safe or not safe for the major allergenes.
Month to month subscription - Create your personalized profile online and get an instant answer if the product is safe or not safe for you. Up to two allergene profiles. Advertisement-free.
Annual subscriptions - Create personalized profile online for you and your family (up to 6 profiles dependant on subscription) and get instant answers if the product is safe for each family member. Advertisement-free.

Credits:

The idea comes from observations in the grocery store: Folks trying to decipher the small print of ingredients and failing, asking other customers for help.
There must be something better.
Observing the restrictions looming allergenes puts on people simply is not acceptable.
There must be something better, a solution.
The idea: Allergen Alarm App.
And for the record: No-one in my family has any allergies.

...about our philosophy, our approach?

We see this project as bottom-up. This means we supply the idea and the concept, the customers and consumers decide if this is a product that is worth having. It will be apparent through backing of the start and the feedback. What comes after the start and the prototype is done, this will need to be seen. It depends on many factors that are at this time not tangible enough.

...about value:

I believe it is worth it, the moment we can save one person from the implications of an allergic reaction because of overlooked food allergens. Additionally, if we can make the lives of only a few dozen people every day a little easier, it is worth it. What do you think? If you agree, please back us, follow us on twitter, recommend us on facebook.

...about future plans:

Our first step is to secure crowd-funding for "bottom-up" developing a prototype application around this idea. The second step is to develop this into a business. Future steps include extension of this idea also for pet food allergens.

Will my name be published if I back this project?
Not by default! If you back (Tier 4 and up) and wish that your name is published as demonstration of your early support for this project, please tell us this. A risk is worth taking if you can live with the worst possible outcome.
